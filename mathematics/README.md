## Overview

Mathematics is organized to build understanding progressively, starting from the most fundamental concepts and advancing to specialized applications.

## Topics

### [01-Foundations](./01-foundations/)
The bedrock of mathematical thinking.
- **Set Theory** - The study of collections of objects
- **Logic** - Formal reasoning and proof structures
- **Proof Techniques** - Methods for establishing mathematical truth

### [02-Number Theory](./02-number-theory/)
Properties and relationships of numbers.
- Prime numbers, divisibility, modular arithmetic
- Diophantine equations
- Cryptographic applications

### [03-Algebra](./03-algebra/)
Structures and operations.
- **Abstract Algebra** - Groups, rings, and fields
- **Linear Algebra** - Vectors, matrices, and linear transformations

### [04-Analysis](./04-analysis/)
The rigorous study of limits, continuity, and change.
- **Calculus** - Differentiation and integration
- **Real Analysis** - Foundations of calculus
- **Complex Analysis** - Functions of complex variables

### [05-Geometry](./05-geometry/)
Properties of space and shapes.
- **Euclidean Geometry** - Classical geometry
- **Differential Geometry** - Geometry using calculus

### [06-Topology](./06-topology/)
Properties preserved under continuous deformations.
- Point-set topology
- Algebraic topology
- Applications in physics

### [07-Probability and Statistics](./07-probability-and-statistics/)
Quantifying uncertainty and analyzing data.
- Probability theory
- Statistical inference
- Stochastic processes

### [08-Applied Mathematics](./08-applied-mathematics/)
Mathematical methods for real-world problems.
- **Numerical Methods** - Computational algorithms
- **Optimization** - Finding best solutions
- **Differential Equations** - Modeling dynamic systems
