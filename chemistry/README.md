## Overview

Chemistry bridges physics and biology, studying matter at the atomic and molecular level. Topics progress from fundamental principles to specialized areas.

## Topics

### [01-General Chemistry](./01-general-chemistry/)
Core principles of chemistry.
- **Atomic Structure** - Electrons, orbitals, and periodicity
- **Chemical Bonding** - Ionic, covalent, and metallic bonds
- **Stoichiometry** - Quantitative relationships in reactions

### [02-Organic Chemistry](./02-organic-chemistry/)
Chemistry of carbon compounds.
- Functional groups
- Reaction mechanisms
- Synthesis strategies
- Stereochemistry

### [03-Inorganic Chemistry](./03-inorganic-chemistry/)
Non-carbon based chemistry.
- Coordination chemistry
- Transition metals
- Main group elements
- Solid-state chemistry

### [04-Physical Chemistry](./04-physical-chemistry/)
Physics applied to chemical systems.
- Chemical thermodynamics
- Kinetics
- Quantum chemistry
- Spectroscopy

### [05-Analytical Chemistry](./05-analytical-chemistry/)
Methods for analyzing substances.
- Spectroscopic methods
- Chromatography
- Electrochemistry
- Mass spectrometry

### [06-Biochemistry](./06-biochemistry/)
Chemistry of living systems.
- Proteins and enzymes
- Nucleic acids
- Metabolism
- Signal transduction

- **Physics**: Quantum mechanics explains bonding; thermodynamics governs reactions
- **Biology**: Biochemistry bridges chemistry and life sciences
- **Mathematics**: Differential equations for kinetics; linear algebra for quantum chemistry
